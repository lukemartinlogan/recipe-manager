# Wild Rice Soup

## Ingredients
- [ ] $wild_rice = 1 cup uncooked wild rice$
- [ ] $butter = 2 tbsp butter$
- [ ] $celery = 2 stalks celery, chopped$
- [ ] $onion = 1 medium yellow onion, chopped$
- [ ] $carrots = 2 large carrots, chopped$
- [ ] $garlic = 4 cloves garlic, minced$
- [ ] $thyme = 1 tsp fresh thyme, stem removed$
- [ ] $vegetable_broth = 3 cups vegetable broth$
- [ ] $evaporated_milk = 1 cup evaporated milk$
- [ ] $salt = salt to taste$

## Instructions
1. Cook $wild_rice$ according to the package directions
2. In a large pot, heat $butter$ over medium heat for 30 seconds or until melted
3. Add $celery$, $onion$, and $carrots$ and cook for five minutes over medium heat, stirring frequently
4. Add $garlic$ and $thyme$ and continue to cook for one minute
5. Add $vegetable_broth$ and bring to a boil
6. Reduce heat to simmer and cook for 15 minutes
7. Remove the thyme stems from the pot
8. Add the cooked $wild_rice$, $evaporated_milk$ and $salt$ (Note: if your rice isn't ready yet, simply turn off the heat of your soup pot and then resume cooking once the rice is ready to be added)
9. Cook over low-medium heat until heated through. If you let this sit before serving, the rice will continue to absorb the broth. Simply add Â½ cup of water and stir until you reach the desired consistency
10. Add additional $salt$ to taste, if needed
